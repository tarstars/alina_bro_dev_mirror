The unexpected: a museum through the eyes of... a teenager!

Part 3. Well... so...

Our museum tour (which we already [told you about](<https://t.me/alina_yerevan_js/398>) ) casually split into three unequal halves. Alina and the guide ran ahead. Our son Vlad and his namesake friend moved slowly and unhurriedly along as their own cozy little group. I ran back and forth.

And now we have a unique opportunity to see what two teenagers, left to their own devices, considered worthy of attention.

A work worthy of the legendary Lefty - eyeglasses with a built-in hearing aid. Today such miniaturization seems self-evident, but 50 years ago it was a real achievement of engineering thought.

A glassy stare, a detached expression, "I'm tired", "when are we going home?" - none of that did we see when the kids reached the stand with inventions by Armenians. With great pleasure they dug into the little cards describing the accordion, an alloy for cymbals, Coca-Cola, paint rollers and the rest.

There it is - parental joy. To see that your child can radiate interest and attention toward something other than a computer and a phone.

Hrazdan. A Non-Tourist City.

Near the museum we wrote about in the previous post, there is [Charles Aznavour Park](<https://yandex.com/maps/-/CLx1A8YM>). The benches painted in Armenian flag colors are from there. Nothing extraordinary, but it is a nice place for a walk with a good city view.

The other two photos are from the [railway station](<https://yandex.com/maps/-/CLx1IJMk>). This is exactly where the city “pearl” is strung on the crooked railway thread. It makes sense that this point is very close to the city’s [central square](<https://yandex.com/maps/-/CLx1ME62>). That is where we picked up the phrase: “Absolutely non-tourist Hrazdan.”

Here is how it happened. We were thinking about a trip souvenir and, as a half-joke, suggested finding a T-shirt saying “I ❤️ Hrazdan.” We entered a small shop near the park and asked two guys around 30 if they had such souvenirs. The response was very emotional, as if we had deeply offended them: “No, we don’t sell that here. Hrazdan is not a tourist city!” Even though they were business owners.

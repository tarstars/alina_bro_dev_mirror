Moving to Yerevan. Part 1

[us](<https://am-lang.web.app/assets/move_to_armenia__preparation-B6ZU7rsr.webp>)

Let’s go back to 2023, when we were preparing to move. This is the first post in the series.

Question one: where to? To stay connected with relatives, we considered only visa-free countries. The first candidate was Serbia. We read channels and articles, exchanged euros, estimated monthly expenses, and reviewed school options. One unpleasant discovery: in winter, air pollution levels on [iqair.com](<iqair.com>) can become very high. Yerevan is not perfect either, but more on that later. We planned to visit Moscow a couple of times per year, and a direct Moscow-Belgrade flight at around 230,000 RUB broke that plan. For Armenia, flights were about 75,000-100,000 RUB for three adults, with many direct options from Russian airlines (Aeroflot, UTair, Ural Airlines, etc.) and Armenian ones (ShirakAvia, FlyOne Armenia). The final step before choosing Armenia was finding a school. We used channels [t.me/ru_schools_armenia](<t.me/ru_schools_armenia>) and [t.me/erevanmoms](<t.me/erevanmoms>).

Here is our relocation checklist:

✍️ Power of attorney for a close relative. Arrange it through a notary. It is needed so someone can represent your interests in organizations. If they should rent out your apartment on your behalf, include that explicitly.
✍️ Withdraw cash rubles to exchange in Armenia. Russian bank cards are generally not accepted, so cash is needed. The best dram/ruble rates are often in SAS exchange points (a Yerevan store chain). You can track rates in Telegram channels, for example [t.me/armeniaCurrency](<t.me/armeniaCurrency>).
✍️ Get a residence permit (VNZh) or plan visa runs. A non-resident can stay in Armenia for 180 days, then a visa run is required. Our residence permit was arranged by an employer. A child under 18 can live in Armenia without crossing the border every six months.
✍️ Choose a school. School selection will be covered in a separate post.
✍️ Choose an apartment. Yerevan and Armenia channels have many rental offers, and [list.am](<list.am>) has a long-term rental section. A downside is many fake realtor listings. We used a realtor recommended by friends.
✍️ Rent out your old apartment. For our Moscow apartment we used Yandex Rent. Advantage: no need to meet tenants in person; you can pick a tenant by profile in the app. There is a contract. Downside: harder to rent out, because tenants pay an additional 7% monthly service fee.
✍️ Set up required services. Install bank apps, Gosuslugi, [mos.ru](<mos.ru>), and the taxpayer account app. All apps worked through VPN.
✍️ Check outstanding obligations: unpaid fines, alimony, etc. Otherwise you may be stopped at the border.
